Surveillance Captialism

By George Varghese

Surveillance capitalism describes a market driven process where the commodity for sale is your personal data, and the capture and production of this data relies on mass surveillance of the internet. This activity is often carried out by companies that provide us with free online services, such as search engines (Google) and social media platforms (Facebook).

The data used in this process is often collected from the same groups of people who will ultimately be its targets. For instance, Google collects personal online data to target us with ads, and Facebook is likely selling our data to organizations who want us to vote for them or to vaccinate our babies. Third parties are also selling our data. These companies buy data from a variety of sources, collate information about individuals or groups of individuals, then sell it.

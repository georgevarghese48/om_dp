Online Privacy Laws

By Michael Teixeira

As technology advances and people become more skilled at using it for their own needs and desires, the potential for crime and breaches of online privacy increase drastically. Although there is no single law that regulates online privacy and any crimes regarding it, there are a variety of federal and state laws that are trying to regulate what they can.

Here are a few federal laws that are helping to regulate online privacy:

The Federal Trade Commission Act

- In 1914, this law established the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), which is the main federal organization that regulates companies with regards to online privacy. The Act was put into place to essentially outlaw unjust methods of competition and acts/practices that affect commerce. Some actions that would trigger a violation of this act include failure to comply with clearly posted privacy policies and failure to properly protect any personal information that the company may be responsible for.

Electronic Communications Privacy Act

- In 1986, this law was enacted to protect various forms of communications by preventing unauthorized government access. Some examples are wire taps of telephones, transmissions of electronic data through computer(s), and oral communications to name a few.

Computer Fraud and Abuse Act

- First enacted in 1986, this act has gone on to be amended a total of 6 times. With each amendment, the list of types of misconduct punishable under this act would grow to reflect the ever-growing field of technology in our modern world. The computer-related activities made unlawful by this act consists of the unauthorized access to a computer with intentions of 1) obtaining sensitive/valuable information, 2) defraud, and 3) to transmit anything harmful.

Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act

- Also known as the Financial Services Modernization Act of 1999, the law was enacted in order to help regulate financial institutions in regards to the handling of personal information of clients. It also requires the institutions to provide customer notices and to have a written information security program in place.

Online Security

By Michael Teixeira

There are a number of things you can do to increase your security while using the internet, varying from basic security measures which should be practiced by everyone ideally, and extreme security measures which as you can guess are extremities that most people can do without. We will stick with the former measures for now so that you may learn and or touch up on the very basic standards of security whilst using the internet. Note that the drawback of increasing your online security generally means giving up on some convenience that is provided. The choice is always yours.

- Make sure you're using secure passwords wherever applicable, including on mobile devices. You can go one step further here and save all your passwords in a secure password manager. To check how secure a password is, click here!

- If you'd like to avoid being tracked, there a number of things you can do but the most basic method is by deleting your browsing history after every session. This includes cleaning out your cookies and cache.

- A highly recommended security measure that anyone can take is installing a VPN (Virtual Private Network). Easy to acquire and user friendly, VPNs are able to hide your identity from Internet Service Providers and anyone else that may be trying to track you.

- Moderate your use of social media. There are some key steps to take here such as to hide any real/personal information because once you put it out into the internet, it's free for anyone to access no matter what. Avoid talking to strangers or just replying to strange/unknown messages, and this goes for emails as well. And for mobile apps, disable unnecessary permissions, such as sharing locations, text messages, photos etc.

- Avoid Javascript as much as you can, or completely disable it within your browser altogether. Javascript is important and can be considered the glue that keeps the Internet together, but it is also integral to the function of cookies. Some recommended search engines that are alternatives to traditional search engines are StartPage, Duck Duck Go, and Firefox to name a few.
